Tangible Events

Events with physical items as scene carriers, the protocol certifies the event itself, not the ownership of physical assets

Core Characteristics of Tangible Events

Physical Items as Scene Carriers

Physical items only serve as physical scene evidence for the occurrence of events, such as first day covers as carriers of "mailing behavior", not the covers themselves.

Multi-source Physical Evidence

Complete physical evidence closed-loop through GPS tracks, on-site images, and third-party vouchers (such as postal receipts) to ensure event authenticity.

On-chain Event Certification

What is ultimately solidified on-chain is the fact that "the event occurred", not the ownership of physical items, avoiding compliance and valuation issues of physical asset on-chain.

Physical Item Perishability

Even if physical items are damaged/lost, the on-chain proven event facts remain valid, and the evidentiary effect does not depend on the survival of physical items.

Typical Application Scenarios

First Day Cover Mailing

Proof of mailing behavior of first day covers and commemorative covers, solidifying core facts such as mailing time, location, addressee/sender.

Art Creation

Proof of art creation process, proving the authenticity and timeline of creation behavior through on-site creation images and material usage records.

Cultural Relic Excavation

Proof of cultural relic excavation events, fixing facts such as excavation time, location, and on-site environment to provide on-chain evidence for cultural relic traceability.

Porcelain Firing

Proof of porcelain firing process, proving core facts such as firing time, kiln, and craftsmanship to provide a basis for craft traceability.
